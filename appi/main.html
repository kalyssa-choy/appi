An Italian Icon: the Vespa

The Vespa was created in 1946 in Pontedera, Italy, after World War 2. Due to the catastrophic state the war left Italy in, automobile and other vehicle manufacturers were struggling to get back on their feet. Roads were destroyed, and Italians needed a new means of transportation. Enrico Piaggio currated the idea for a 2 wheeled vehicle that would be reliable and inexpensive to make. The Piaggio family was involved in the aerospace industry, so the first vespa was made from spare aircraft parts. Corradino D'Ascanio designed the vehicle, and due to his unique design and its sound, it was named "Vespa" (Italian for wasp). It quickly gained popularity throughout Europe, and made its way all around the world. The Vespa is not only a popular means of transport, but also is a representation of the fruit of life and a symbol of fashion.
